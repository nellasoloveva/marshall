MARSHALL HISTORY
A young Jim Marshall poses with a drum kit embossed with his own name on the front.
MANY YEARS BEFORE
James Charles Marshall, discovered his musical talents through tap dancing at a young age. Instaed of focusing on traditional workschool, he pursued a career in music as singer and drummer. By the late 1930s, he was perfoming semi-profesionally and teaching drum students in the late 1940s, many of whom went on to play with famous musicians.
"JIM MARSHALL AND SON"
On 7 July 1960, after over 20 years of gigging, Jim with his wife Violet and son Terry, opened "Jim Marshall and Son" in London.
A plaque can be found along the road today, commemorating the Marshall brand's humble beginnings.
Iconic bands like The Beatles, The Rolling Stones, Led Zeppelin and Deep Purple embraced the raw power of Marshall amps, contributing to a sonic revolution that defined the era and solidified Marshall's status as the amplifier of choise the rock legends.
"NUMBER ONE"
Jim and Terry Marshall, along with their service engineer Ken Bran, began to fashion together prototypes for a thunderous new amplification device, initially expirimenting with military components left over from the Second World War. Eventually, the team - guided by the saxophonist's ears of Terry - landed on a design that would later become know as "NUMBER ONE".
Marshall took over London's Wembley Arena for a musical blow-out in September 2012 to commemorate an incredible milestone.
In 2010, Marshall entered the lifestyle products market, aiming to bring its renowned sound and craftsmanship from the stage into the hands of millions. Since then, the company has been producing prtable and homes speakers, as well as three types of headphones.
